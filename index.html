Adolescent Nutrition Reporting

This application was developed with the main purpose of helping teachers to track down who are students that need nutrition education.

Healthy children learn better. People with adequate nutrition are more productive and can gradually create opportunities to break the cycles of poverty and hunger. Malnutrition, in every form, presents significant threats to human health. Today the world faces a double burden of malnutrition that includes both undernutrition and overweight, especially in low- and middle-income countries.

Key Health Concerns Addressed:

  • Iron Deficiency Anemia: Now the number one cause of lost disability-adjusted life years in adolescent girls globally, with three major consequences: decreased school performance, loss of productivity, and decreased reproductive health.
  • Worm Infections: Affecting more than one-third of the world's population, with the most intense infections in children and the poor, leading to long-term negative effects on health, nutrition, cognitive development, and education.
  • BMI Monitoring: Tracking Body Mass Index to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

System Features:

  • Teachers can add students class-wise and create participation lists for various programs
  • Generate weekly, monthly, and yearly reports easily
  • Refer students with nutrition problems via downloadable PDF forms
  • Track WIFA tablets and Deworming tablets inventory and usage
  • Calculate BMI to identify students needing nutrition intervention
  • Access nutrition education modules in PDF format (available offline)
  • User-friendly interface for adding students and teachers manually
  • Monitor class participation in Nutrition Programs
  • Works in both online and offline modes
